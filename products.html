NGUVU COFFEE

We produce high quality ground and whole roasted coffee beans produced by local Ugandan farmers. Our coffee comes from two distinct plants, Arabic and Robusta, which are native to Uganda and rich in flavour.
The company has four different types, Ordinary Nguvu coffee, Factory Fresh Coffee, Classic Coffee and Roasted Coffee Beans.

1. Ordinary Nguvu Coffee

This coffee is processed from dark roasted Arabica coffee beans that are finely ground. It is very sweet in flavour with a bitter element. The coffee is grown in mountainous regions due to their access to fresh water and fertile soils. These coffee beans are supplied by farmers from central and western parts of Ugandan including Mbale (Mount Elgon) and Kasese (Rwenzori Mountain Range).

BIOMASS BRIQUETTE

Biomass Briquettes are clean burning fuels capable of producing long lasting heat for efficient cooking and heating. Agro-waste materials, from sawdust to coffee husks, are ground, heated and compressed to produce our high value solid fuel briquette.
Our biomass briquettes have a density of around 650kg/m3 with a diameter of 60mm. They are highly acceptable to institutional kitchens and can burn directly in existing stoves with minor modifications.

High Fuel Efficiency

Our briquettes burn with greater heat energy compared to traditional fuels. They burn for a longer period of time and thus is are more economical fuel for our customers.


Improves your Health

This clean fuel burns with smaller flames and less smoke emissions compared to firewood. Creating a more hygienic cooking environment and reducing the risk of health problems for the users.


Saves the Environment

Uganda’s natural resources are under pressure. Using organic materials to produce a clean-burning fuel helps stop widespread deforestation, reduces carbon emissions and decreases excessive waste.


Benefits of the Biomass Briquettes…

  • Briquettes are a good substitute for wood and charcoal
  • Clean burning with a small flame and less smoke
  • Easy to handle and to ignite
  • Reduced manual labour as Briquette do not require chopping
  • Cook with an even heat and are longer lasting
  • More suitable for slow cooking and difficult to ignite

Proven a popular alternative to fossil fuels in North America, Europe and India. Now, the demand for biomass briquettes in Uganda is growing – NGUVU promises to meet this demand.
