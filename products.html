NGUVU COFFEE

Kampala Jellitone Suppliers is an eco-friendly company located in Kampala, Uganda. We specialise in manufacturing three quality products: Roasted Coffee, Biomass Briquettes and Energy-saving Stoves for people irrespective of their income. Our products sustain and cultivate the rich environment of Uganda.


Our History


Kampala Jellitone Suppliers Ltd (KJS) was established in 1981 under the Company's Act and later registered under the factories in 1985. The company initially produced petroleum jelly products for the Ugandan market. In 1984, the company shifted into coffee roasting. The company later embarked on the production of non-carbonised fuel briquettes, a technology they soucred from Denmark with assistance of the Danish private Sector Development Programme.

Our Team


Managing Director

The company was founded by Haji Abasi Kazibwe Musisi, who is an experienced banker, formerly working for Standard Chartered Bank Uganda. He co-ordinates all business activities and the research at the Fuels from Waste Research Centre.

Our Future
Kampala Jellitone Suppliers has the capactiy to produce 2000 tonnes of briquettes per year. The company's future ambitions include relocating to a larger factory, equipped with more machinery to scale up Biomass Briquette production, for expansion into beyond Uganda into the Easter African market.

OUR PRODUCTS

Coffee

We produce quality Ground Coffee and Roasted Beans sourced from local Ugandan farmers. Our medium to dark roast coffee is strong with refreshing fruity overtones.


Biomass Briquette

We produce Biomass Briquettes from agricultural residues and sawdust. This fuel is a viable and low cost alternative to environmentally damaging fuels such as firewood, kerosene and charcoal.


Briquette Stove

We produce briquette stoves that consume less fuel compared to traditional stoves, saving money, time and resources. Our stoves guarantee increased health and safety benefits.

Our modern and highly efficient Energy-Saving Stoves not only conserve the amount of fuel consumed but also promotes a clean, cool and smoke free cooking environment for the users of the briquettes.
"Particulates generated from this incomplete combustion of firewood are dangerous for one's health - Biomass Briquettes are cleaner and smoke free" - Abasi K.Musisi, KJS Managing Director.

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT

Curbs Deforestation

Trees in Uganda are being spared from exploitation as a readily-available fuel source as instutions and industries start uding Biomass Briquette. The Biomass Business project relies on agricultural waste materials, which has no further useful purpose, to create an alternative source of fuel

Limits CO2 Emissions

Biomass Briquettes combustion has reduced CO2 emissions compared to the burning of charcoal and firewood. Using the residue-based Briquettes instead traditional fuels saves approximately 6.1 tonnes of CO2 per tonne of Briquette
